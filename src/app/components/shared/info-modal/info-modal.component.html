WELCOME TO THE FACTOM DID REGISTRAR

The Factom Decentralized Identifier (DID) registrar is a UI, which walks you through the steps of creating and managing a digital identity.

Your digital identity contains publicly discoverable information about you, which can be used by anyone when they try to:

  • communicate with you
  • authenticate you
  • authorize you for access to some resource

In addition to this, a digital identity enables you to digitally sign messages, so that you can prove that a given message comes from you, but also that a different message did not originate from you. This means that no one will be able to falsify a statement you made, or claim that you wrote something when you did not.

Digital identities are not limited to people. They could be used to represent just about anything: a computer or a device on a network, your pet, a parcel, or even some paper document.

The digital identity you are about to create or modify consists of two components: a public and a private one. The public part will be recorded on the Factom blockchain and will contain relevant metadata for the identity, which is useful for any party that wants to interact with the subject sitting behind the digital identity. The private part will be stored in an encrypted file that you must download and keep safely stored.

You are now ready to start! Make sure to keep an eye for the tooltips along the way, as they will guide you through the procedure.
